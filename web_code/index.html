Introducing CLASHub 1.0, a comprehensive and user-friendly platform that combines a rich database of CLASH results with advanced tools for data analysis. CLASHub provides direct access to cross-linking ligation and sequencing of hybrids (CLASH) data, revealing microRNA-target interactions by utilizing UV cross-linking to covalently bind microRNAs and their target RNAs within the Argonaute protein complex. The database includes data for four model organisms: Human, Mouse, Drosophila melanogaster, and Caenorhabditis elegans. It features both wild-type and ZSWIM8 knockout samples—a gene involved in mediating Target-Directed microRNA Degradation (TDMD)—providing valuable insights into microRNA regulation. CLASHub not only offers thoroughly analyzed and newly generated CLASH data but also includes gene and microRNA expression data from various cellular contexts, supporting researchers in their investigations into the intricate regulatory roles of miRNAs in health and disease.

Beyond serving as a database, CLASHub offers a user-friendly Analyzer tool for conducting simple, fast, and in-depth analyses, including CLASH data processing, miRNA-seq analyses, RNA-seq analyses, and cumulative fraction curve analyses. These capabilities enable detailed exploration of miRNA-target hybrids, precise identification of miRNA binding sites within genes, and comprehensive analysis of how miRNAs influence target expression levels. By integrating high-quality data with robust analytical tools, CLASHub facilitates comparative studies across multiple organisms, advancing the understanding of miRNA-mediated gene regulation in health and disease.
